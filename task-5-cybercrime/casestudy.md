# Case Study: UPI Payment Fraud (QR Code Scam)

### What is the Crime?
UPI Payment Fraud, specifically the "QR Code Scam," is a type of financial cybercrime where fraudsters trick victims into scanning a QR code to *receive* money, but in reality, money is *deducted* from the victim's account.

### How it Happens (Step-by-Step):
1. **The Hook:** The fraudster contacts the victim via OLX or Facebook Marketplace, pretending to be a buyer interested in an item the victim is selling.
2. **The Trust:** They agree to the price immediately to gain trust and say they will pay via UPI.
3. **The Trap:** The fraudster sends a QR code on WhatsApp and tells the victim, "Scan this to receive the payment."
4. **The Theft:** When the victim scans the code and enters their UPI PIN, they unknowingly authorize a debit transaction.
5. **The Loss:** Money is instantly deducted from the victim's bank account, and the fraudster switches off their phone.

### Who is Targeted?
Usually, students, home-sellers, and small business owners who are frequently using UPI for quick transactions are the primary targets.

### Consequences:
Victims suffer immediate financial loss, ranging from a few thousands to lakhs of rupees. Beyond money, it leads to a loss of trust in digital payments and significant mental stress.
